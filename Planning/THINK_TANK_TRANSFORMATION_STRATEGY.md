# China Watch Think Tank Transformation Strategy

**Created:** January 24, 2026
**Status:** Strategic Planning
**Objective:** Transform China Watch from "intelligence terminal" aesthetic to credible Think Tank positioning

---

## 1. POSITIONING STATEMENT

### Current State
- "Binary intelligence terminal" aesthetic
- Feels like a tech/hacker tool
- Content labeled as "intelligence" and "signals"

### Target State
**"China Watch is an independent research institute providing rigorous analysis on China's economy, policy, and geopolitical impact for policymakers, business leaders, and informed citizens."**

### Competitive Positioning
| Think Tank | Focus | China Watch Differentiation |
|------------|-------|----------------------------|
| MERICS | Europe-China relations | Global perspective, AI-enhanced research |
| CSIS | US security policy | Accessible to general audience, not just DC |
| Brookings | Academic depth | Faster, more actionable insights |
| ASPI | Indo-Pacific security | Economic + political integration |

**China Watch Niche:** *Accessible, timely, AI-augmented China analysis for the informed global citizen*

---

## 2. CONTENT TYPE HIERARCHY

Based on [think tank best practices](https://onthinktanks.org/reports/), establish a clear content hierarchy:

### Tier 1: Flagship Publications (Monthly/Quarterly)
| Type | Description | Length | Example Title |
|------|-------------|--------|---------------|
| **Annual Report** | "State of China" comprehensive review | 50-100 pages | *China 2026: The Year in Review* |
| **Research Reports** | Deep-dive original research | 15-30 pages | *China's Semiconductor Strategy: A Five-Year Assessment* |
| **Special Reports** | Timely analysis of major events | 10-20 pages | *The Taiwan Strait Crisis: Implications for Global Trade* |

### Tier 2: Regular Analysis (Weekly)
| Type | Description | Length | Frequency |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Policy Briefs** | Actionable analysis for decision-makers | 2-4 pages | Weekly |
| **Economic Monitor** | Data-driven economic tracking | 3-5 pages | Weekly |
| **Sector Analysis** | Industry-specific deep dives | 5-10 pages | Bi-weekly |

### Tier 3: Rapid Response (Daily/As-needed)
| Type | Description | Length | Turnaround |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| **Commentary** | Expert opinion on breaking news | 800-1500 words | 24-48 hours |
| **Quick Takes** | First reactions to developments | 300-500 words | Same day |
| **Data Snapshots** | Visualized data points | Infographic | Real-time |

## 5. CREDIBILITY SIGNALS

### Must-Have Elements

#### A. Methodology Page
```markdown
## Our Research Methodology

### Data Sources
- Official Chinese government statistics (NBS, MOFCOM, PBOC)
- International organizations (IMF, World Bank, WTO)
- Proprietary data collection and analysis
- Academic and peer-reviewed research

### Quality Assurance
- All publications undergo internal review
- Key findings verified against multiple sources
- Methodology disclosed for reproducibility

### AI-Assisted Research
China Watch employs AI tools to:
- Monitor and translate Chinese-language sources
- Identify patterns in large datasets
- Accelerate initial research phases

All AI outputs are reviewed and verified by human analysts.
```

#### B. Funding & Independence Statement
```markdown
## Our Independence

China Watch is committed to intellectual independence. We do not accept:
- Funding from any government
- Donations that restrict our editorial freedom
- Sponsored content disguised as research

Our funding sources are disclosed annually.
```

#### C. Expert/Author Profiles
Each publication should include:
- Author name and credentials
- Professional headshot
- Brief bio
- Contact information
- Previous publications

#### D. Citation Standards
- Academic-style citations
- Linked sources
- Data provenance statements
- Downloadable PDF versions
